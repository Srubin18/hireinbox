## STRENGTHS (Pushing Score Up)

1. **Strong market fit** - High unemployment (33%+), POPIA compliance needs, underserved creator economy
2. **Core innovation** - Evidence-based AI with "receipts" = smart differentiator for trust
3. **SA context moat** - Global tools ignore CA(SA), Big 4 articles, local nuances
4. **Multi-product approach** - B2B, B2C, B2Creator diversifies revenue
5. **Modern tech stack** - Next.js, Supabase, GPT-4o = scalable, cost-effective
6. **MVP execution** - Shows capability even with AI CTO
7. **Unit economics** - High margins, low CAC via organic/referrals

---

## WEAKNESSES (Pulling Score Down)

1. **Pre-revenue risk** - Traction is synthetic data/prototypes, not real users/revenue
2. **OpenAI dependency** - Could spike costs, downtime, hallucination issues
3. **Aggressive creator projections** - 500K unrealistic; SA has 50K-100K active creators
4. **Lean team** - Efficient but risky for scaling sales/ops
5. **Competitive threat** - LinkedIn could adapt quickly with resources

## HONEST CREATOR ASSESSMENT

### What's Strong
- Passport = clever entry point against $2B fraud problem
- Video coaching with timestamps is unique
- R49 one-time pricing lowers barriers
- SA context is real moat

### What's Weak
- Too passive - creators want ongoing value, not static badge
- 500K target unrealistic - SA has <100K active creators
- Competition (Linktree/Beacons) is free
- GPT-4o may struggle with Zulu/Xhosa accents

### The Fix
> Position as "AI Manager" not just verification tool

**Evolution path:**
1. Creator Passport (R49 one-time) → Entry
2. Creator Scorecard (dynamic benchmarks) → Engagement
3. Pro Inbox (R99/mo) → Retention + Revenue
